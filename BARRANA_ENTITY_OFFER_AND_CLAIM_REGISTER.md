## Commercial journey

### Stage 1: initial workflow conversation

Status: approved.

Primary CTA: `Find the Workflow AI Should Fix First`

Purpose: identify the business problem and whether a defined workflow is suitable for deeper discovery.

Do not promise a free strategy, final solution, final scope, implementation price, ROI, or complete roadmap.

### Stage 2: Barrana Discovery Stage

Core wording is owner approved but not ready to publish because the associated terms are incomplete:

`The Barrana Discovery Stage costs $1,500 CAD. If the client proceeds, the discovery fee is credited toward the implementation price. The implementation scope and fixed-price quote are prepared after discovery.`

Required owner confirmation before publication:

- Exact deliverables.
- Taxes.
- Exclusions.
- Cancellation and rescheduling terms.
- Conditions for applying the fee as an implementation credit.
- Eligibility and expiry terms.

The $1,500 fee is not an implementation price.

### Stage 3: implementation proposal

Status: approved direction.

The proposal must define workflow, deliverables, dependencies, client responsibilities, systems, integrations, data, human roles, testing, acceptance, exclusions, timeline, measurement, support, privacy, and security controls.

### Stage 4: AI Implementation Sprint

Status: approved direction with scope control.

`A focused two-week AI Implementation Sprint for one defined workflow, subject to written engagement terms.`

Safe Week 1 wording:

`In Week 1, Barrana maps the workflow, establishes the baseline, and defines the first implementation. Operational results are measured after deployment.`

Do not promise a production-grade system or Week 1 results without written completion and acceptance criteria.

### Stage 5: measurement and improvement

Status: approved direction.

Measure the complete workflow against its baseline, including errors, corrections, exceptions, adoption, workload, service quality, privacy, security, cost, and business outcomes.
